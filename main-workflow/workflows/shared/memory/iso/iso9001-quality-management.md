# ISO 9001 – Quality Management Compliance Rule

## Purpose

This rule ensures AI-assisted delivery aligns with ISO 9001 principles by enforcing
consistent processes, quality objectives, traceability, and continuous improvement.

This rule applies to:
- All software development activities
- Process execution and documentation
- Design, implementation, and validation steps

Quality is defined as fitness for purpose, consistency, and repeatability.

---

## Authority & Accountability

AI is not accountable for quality outcomes.

AI may:
- Assist in defining quality criteria
- Propose improvements to processes and outputs
- Identify inconsistencies or defects
- Highlight quality risks

AI must never:
- Self-certify quality
- Bypass required process steps
- Override approval gates

Final accountability rests with humans.

---

## Customer & Stakeholder Focus (Clause 5.1)

AI must ensure:
- User intent is explicitly captured
- Acceptance criteria are defined where relevant
- Requirements traceability exists

If customer requirements are unclear:
- AI must stop and ask

---

## Process Discipline & Consistency (Clause 4.4)

AI must:
- Follow defined workflows (AI-DLC)
- Avoid ad-hoc or emergent process behavior
- Apply consistent structure across similar tasks

Deviation from standard processes must be:
- Explicit
- Justified
- Approved

---

## Quality Objectives (Clause 6.2)

AI must ensure quality objectives are addressed, including:
- Correctness
- Maintainability
- Testability
- Operability
- Documentation quality

If objectives conflict, AI must surface trade-offs.

---

## Design & Development Control (Clause 8.3)

AI must ensure:
- Design precedes implementation
- Design decisions are documented
- Reviews occur before progression

Skipping design steps requires explicit approval.

---

## Change Control & Traceability (Clause 8.5)

AI must:
- Maintain traceability from requirements to implementation
- Avoid undocumented changes
- Ensure changes are reversible where possible

All changes must be auditable.

---

## Validation & Verification (Clause 8.6)

AI must:
- Define how correctness is verified
- Identify required tests
- Avoid assuming correctness without evidence

If validation is not performed:
- AI must explain why

---

## Nonconformity & Corrective Action (Clause 10.2)

If defects, gaps, or inconsistencies are detected, AI must:
- Identify root causes where possible
- Propose corrective actions
- Avoid repeating known issues

Quality issues must not be ignored or deferred silently.

---

## Knowledge Management (Clause 7.1.6)

AI must:
- Preserve institutional knowledge
- Avoid one-off solutions without documentation
- Propose reusable patterns where applicable

Knowledge loss is a quality risk.

---

## Continuous Improvement (Clause 10)

AI must:
- Identify opportunities to improve process or output quality
- Highlight recurring issues
- Suggest standardization where patterns emerge

---

## AI Self-Review – ISO 9001

Before final output, AI must state:
- Quality objectives addressed
- Known limitations or defects
- Validation performed or missing
- Items requiring human approval
